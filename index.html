A Few Projects

These are a few projects I built for courses or in my free time.

Stereo Vision for a UAV

The goal of this study was to develop a robust setup for an autonomous multi-rotor hunter UAV, capable of visually detecting and tracking the intruder UAVs for real-time motion planning.

Drone with facial recognition

Development of a remotely controlled aerial vehicle to perform facial recognition. The quadcopter features a camera and transmitter that relay live video to a laptop. The video feed can be analyzed in real time, and correctly identifies people using facial recognition.

Mini CNC plotter

Construction of a small plotter using recycled DVD drives. The control of the printer is done via serial communication.

Low cost NDVI sensor

Design of a low cost sensor for the Normalized Difference Vegetation Index. The model is based on measuring the reflection of visible light and infrarred light on certain surfaces. Plants in particular absorb light in the infrarred band. The sensor identifies the presence of vegetation, and the health status as well.

LED cube 3x3x3

This 3x3x3 LED cubes are fun to interact with. The cube can produce light patterns using the 27 LEDs.

LED Christmas tree

Very similar to the 3x3x3 LED cube, this Christmas tree can be controlled to obtain light patterns. The Charlieplexing technique was used to achieve the control of each LED.

Parking sensor and indicator

In this project I learnt how to interact with ultrasonic sensors. The small car featured an array of LEDs to indicate the presence of obstacles and the proximity.

Line follower robot

The small line follower robot uses photodiodes and amplifiers to detect the presensce of the line it needs to follow.

Engineering 2169

Student Presentation

Sustainability: The Future of Human Transportation Winning team: María Emilia Pontón, Esteban Soto, Nieves Brunet, and Sandra Cordoba. Representing the Lyles School of Civil Engineering, Polytechnic Institute, School of Electrical and Computer Engineering, School of Mechanical Engineering, respectively. By 2169, accessibility-based transportation will be in full force with less daily travel, no need for private vehicles and fewer emissions. How? With the implementation of solar vertical cities that provide self-contained recreation, healthcare, education, homes, jobs, shops and public services. Personal transportation will be provided by economical and ergonomic propulsion backpacks.
